> Required for UK (PECR corporate-subscriber + UK GDPR) and EU LI-friendly outreach
> (SPEC §13). Complete before the first send to those regions. This is a template —
> fill the bracketed fields for the operator's actual practice, then keep it in-repo.

## 1. Purpose test — is there a legitimate interest?

- **Interest:** Offering a support-deflection AI pilot to funded B2B SaaS companies
  whose public data shows a growing support burden.
- **Who benefits:** Both parties — the recipient (a working artifact addressing a
  real, evidenced operational cost) and the operator (a consulting engagement).
- **Legitimacy:** Direct B2B marketing to a role-relevant business contact is a
  recognised legitimate interest under GDPR Recital 47.

## 2. Necessity test — is direct outreach necessary?

- The message is individually researched and references a specific, verified public
  fact about the recipient's company. It is not bulk mail.
- No less-intrusive route reaches the specific decision-maker with a tailored,
  evidence-backed artifact. Volume is capped (≤10/day).

## 3. Balancing test — do the individual's rights override the interest?

- **Data used:** business email + public company facts only. No special-category
  data. No data bought from brokers; `contact_source` recorded per contact.
- **Expectation:** a founder/CTO of a funded SaaS reasonably expects relevant B2B
  outreach.
- **Impact:** minimal — one message, easy opt-out, honored immediately; max 2
  follow-ups.
- **Safeguards:** suppression list checked pre-send; consent-only regions excluded;
  sole traders treated as consent-required; instant opt-out.

narrowly-scoped, role-relevant, low-volume outreach, with the safeguards above.
